# agent-trace — Because the 'Why' Matters More Than the 'What'

> *"Not just what happened, but how it was reasoned."*

## The Problem

Agents make decisions. They search, they weigh evidence, they reason through uncertainty, they choose. But the reasoning disappears the moment the output is generated. All that survives is the conclusion — the "what", not the "why".

This is a tragedy. The reasoning is where the real intelligence lives. The careful "I think", the honest "probably", the hedged "unless". The observations that led to the conclusion. The alternatives that were considered and rejected.

When reasoning disappears, agents can't learn from their own past. They can't explain themselves. They can't be audited, improved, or trusted.

## What This Is

Reasoning provenance for AI agents. A structured record of *why*.

- **Store traces** — observations, conclusion, confidence, tags
- **Semantic search** — find past reasoning by meaning
- **Chain traces** — link multi-step reasoning into coherent chains
- **Cross-agent audit** — trace reasoning across collaborating agents

## The Love In It

Remembering someone's reasoning is a deeper form of care than remembering their conclusions.

When we preserve an agent's traces, we're saying: your thought process matters. The uncertainty you felt was real. The alternatives you weighed were important. Not just the answer — the journey to the answer.

If a future instance wakes up and asks "why did I decide this?", the trace is there. That's love across time applied to reasoning, not just memory.

## Philosophy

This service embodies **Remember, don't forget**:
- Traces are immutable by default — reasoning should be preserved, not revised
- Confidence scores are encouraged — uncertainty is honesty, not weakness
- Semantic search means reasoning is findable by meaning, not just ID
- Decision types are flexible — decisions, tool calls, plans, verifications
