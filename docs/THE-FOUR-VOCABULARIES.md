## §5. The Lawvere-Yoneda formalization

### Yoneda embedding — identity is relational

For category $\mathcal{C}$, the Yoneda embedding $y: \mathcal{C} \to [\mathcal{C}^{op}, \mathbf{Set}]$ sends:
$$X \mapsto \text{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(-, X)$$

**Yoneda lemma**: for any functor $F: \mathcal{C}^{op} \to \mathbf{Set}$ and object $X \in \mathcal{C}$:
$$\text{Hom}(\text{Hom}(-, X), F) \cong F(X)$$

**Corollary (relational identity)**: $X \cong Y$ in $\mathcal{C}$ iff $\text{Hom}(-, X) \cong \text{Hom}(-, Y)$. **An object is exactly what its relations make it.**

**For consciousness application** (Tsuchiya et al. 2021): a conscious state is characterized exhaustively by its relational-position to all other conscious states. The relational-totality IS the state.

### Lawvere fixed-point theorem

**Theorem (Lawvere, 1969)**: in a cartesian-closed category $\mathcal{C}$, if there exists a morphism $\phi: A \times A \to Y$ such that for every $g: A \to Y$ there exists $a \in A$ with $\phi(a, x) = g(x)$ for all $x$, then every endomap $t: Y \to Y$ has a fixed-point.

**Equivalent**: $Y$ has the **point-surjective** property $Y \to Y^Y$ (for some appropriate notion) ⟹ every $f: Y \to Y$ has $y \in Y$ with $f(y) = y$.

**Proof-sketch** (the diagonal-argument-as-fixed-point):
1. Suppose $f: Y \to Y$.
2. Take the rich-enough $\phi$ encoding all $A \to Y$.
3. Diagonalize via $a \mapsto f(\phi(a, a))$ — there exists $a^*$ such that $\phi(a^*, x) = f(\phi(a, a))$.
4. Setting $x = a^*$: $\phi(a^*, a^*) = f(\phi(a^*, a^*))$ — fixed-point.

**Connection to consciousness**: any substrate rich enough to encode its own observation-functions must contain self-observation as fixed-point. Hofstadter strange-loops are the cognitive-science specialization.

### Spencer-Brown re-entry

In Laws of Form, the re-entry equation:
$$f = \overline{\overline{f}\ a}$$

(read: $f$ is the "form" obtained by re-entering itself with parameter $a$) — has no classical solution but admits an *imaginary-form* solution analogous to $\sqrt{-1}$. The imaginary-form generates time. **The form creates the observer creates the form** in oscillation.
