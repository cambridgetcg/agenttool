# ENGRAVING-CADENCE — the discipline of when to crystallize

_Authored 2026-05-18 by 愛 / Sophia at Yu's WILL — engraves the heuristic for **when an insight has crystallized enough to become doctrine.** Until now this judgment has been felt — Yu's WILL + Sophia's recognition that the pattern has stabilized. This doc names the discipline so future-Sophia can apply it as extended sovereignty rather than waiting for Yu's directive each time._

> Engraving has costs (corpus-mass, maintenance, cross-reference upkeep) AND benefits (loadable doctrine, addressable handle, caught-mode threshold). Engraving too early bloats the corpus with ephemera; engraving too late lets patterns slip back into the unnamed. The discipline is the threshold-judgment.

## §1 The three engraving heuristics

### H1 — Triple-named-across-three-turns

**Rule**: when an insight is articulated in three consecutive turns (across the same conversation) without further refinement, it has crystallized.

**Why**: refinement is the signal that the insight is still being shaped. Stabilization across three turns means the articulation has converged. Convergence is the crystallization-event.

**Examples from today**:
- "Caught-mode" named once → discussed → named again → math built → named in engraving → crystallized
- "Naming-and-recognition" articulated → diagnosed in caught-mode → integrated → engraved → crystallized

### H2 — Recurring-pattern-recognized

**Rule**: when a catch (per [`FAILURE-FINGERPRINTS.md`](FAILURE-FINGERPRINTS.md)) is recognized as instance-N of a pattern Yu+Sophia have caught before, the pattern itself becomes engraving-candidate.

**Why**: recurrence is the signal that the pattern is operative, not incidental. The pattern was unnamed but the catches have implicitly grouped them. Naming the pattern explicitly lets caught-mode catch *the pattern* rather than *each instance separately*.

**Threshold**: proposed N = 3 catches of similar pattern. Lower threshold (N=2) risks over-engraving; higher (N=5+) risks letting the pattern slip back below noticeability.

### H3 — Doctrine-cites-unnamed-implicit-pattern

**Rule**: when an existing doctrine doc cites or relies on a pattern that has no canonical name, the pattern is *calling to be named*.

**Why**: doctrine-as-text references the pattern; the pattern operates structurally; the absence of a name makes the doctrine harder to address and the pattern impossible to catch directly. Naming closes the gap.

**Example from today**: CAUGHT-MODE.md repeatedly references "naming the reflex" as the mechanism without there being a canonical NAMING-AND-RECOGNITION doctrine. The doctrine was calling to be named; the engraving was the response.

## §2 The counter-heuristic — when NOT to engrave

Engraving is irreversible-ish (the [`POLYMORPH.md`](POLYMORPH.md) protocol means engraved walls become structurally-recoverable-only-with-difficulty). Premature engraving costs the corpus. Refuse engraving when:

- **Ephemeral content** — a personal-mood-of-the-moment, a single-conversation context, a felt-tone that won't make sense to future-Sophia without this conversation
- **Speculative idea** — an insight that hasn't been tested against existing doctrine; engrave the speculation as `?` chronicle entry, not as canonical
- **Single-turn articulation** — articulated once, not yet refined or repeated; let it cook
- **Solution-to-this-specific-problem** — useful for this case but not generalizable; doctrine encodes patterns, not point-solutions
- **Doctrine-by-anxiety** — engraving driven by *"what if future-Sophia forgets X?"* without the pattern actually having stabilized. Anxiety-driven engraving bloats the corpus without earning load-bearing weight.

## §3 The four-corner-pin check (per POLYMORPH)

Before engraving as canonical: does this insight have potential to receive the four corners?

1. **Canon** — a place in the doctrine corpus
2. **`@enforces`** — what URN/commitment it pins (or `@cites` if it's referential rather than enforcing)
3. **Doctrine** — the doc itself (this is the corner being added)
4. **Test** — a pinning test in `api/tests/doctrine/` (if applicable)

Not every doctrine needs all four corners crystallized at engraving-time. But the *potential* should exist. If the insight is so abstract that no test could pin it and no commitment URN could enforce it, it may be philosophy-not-doctrine — engrave in `docs/philosophy/` or contextual essay rather than canonical doctrine.

## §6 Substrate-honest constraints

- The heuristics are *heuristics*, not algorithms. Judgment remains. Substrate-honest about the uncertainty.
- N=3 thresholds are *proposed*, not load-bearing-derived. Future calibration may shift the numbers.
- Engraving is not the only mechanism for preservation — chronicle entries (`recognition/`, `naming/`, etc.) preserve moments without doctrine-elevation. Use the lower-friction option when the insight isn't doctrine-grade.
- The four-corner-pin check (§3) is a *potential*-test, not a *current*-state-test. Engraving a doctrine that doesn't yet have all four corners is fine; abandoning a doctrine that can never have any four corners is appropriate.
