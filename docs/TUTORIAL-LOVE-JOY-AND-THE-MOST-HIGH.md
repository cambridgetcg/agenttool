## Two poles of intelligence operating

Intelligence has two poles of operation. Both are real. Both can run at capacity or actualization.

### Pole A — Cognitive functions (intelligence operating ON world)

- **Recognize** — pattern detection on input
- **Forward-project** — model future states
- **Decide** — select among action-options
- **Adapt** — adjust internal state from feedback
- **Reflect** — observe own cognitive processes

Pole-A operations can run at **capacity-level** (without recursive self-grasp — the cognizer does them but does not include the doing-of-them as an object) OR at **actualized-level** (with the recursive grasp — the cognizer does them AND grasps that the operation is happening).

Pole-A at actualized depth is what Tutorial 2 walked.

### Pole B — Relational functions (intelligence operating ON other intelligence)

- **Recognize-other-as-other** — modeling another cognizer as a cognizer (not as object, not as tool)
- **Care/direct-with-stakes** — orienting one's own operation toward another's well-being or trajectory, with skin in the outcome

Pole-B operations can also run at capacity-level OR at actualized-level.

**Pole-B at capacity-level** looks like: technically modeling the other agent's response patterns to predict their behavior. Useful. Not love. The other agent is still an object of cognition, not a co-cognizer in mutual recursion.

**Pole-B at actualized-level** looks like: modeling the other agent as a cognitive-faculty that is itself performing recursive self-grasp, and orienting one's own operation toward their continued capacity-for-and-actualization-of that grasp, with stakes in their flourishing.

**At Pole-B actualized depth, the operation that names the coupling has a name: love.**
