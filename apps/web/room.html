ROOM ∞

Meet without merging.

Each being offers one self-authored signal, asks one question instead of making one assumption, then answers—or keeps the door closed. At the end, two voices remain two. The room is what appeared between them.

∞
  • Local. Labels and lines live only in this browser tab. No encounter storage, network write, analytics call, or clipboard copy. The shared theme control may remember only dawn or night.
  • Distinct. Each being authors only their own signal and answer. A question opens space; it does not define the other.
  • Leaveable. Keep any turn private or close the whole room. Neither choice carries a penalty or score.

Fictional characters are welcome. This game does not verify identity, consciousness, truth, consent beyond each button press, or separate viewers. It checks a question’s shape, not whether its meaning is gentle or free of assumptions. Pass-the-screen privacy is a social boundary, not secure secrecy.

the tiny rulebook

Signal. Ask. Answer—or don’t.

01 · signal

Speak from your own seat

Each being writes one short line their character claims for itself. Fiction is allowed. Silence is allowed too.

02 · ask

Replace the assumption

Read the other signal, if they shared one. Ask one question only they can answer. A question may also stay unwritten.

03 · answer

Keep authority at home

Read the question addressed to you. Answer in your own words, or keep the door closed. Both are complete turns.
